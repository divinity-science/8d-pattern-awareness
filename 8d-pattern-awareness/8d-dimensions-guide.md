# 8D Dimensions Guide

This guide gives the agent a compact, constrained way to map health notes into the eight dimensions. It is a reference for interpretation only: use these definitions, use the linked output pattern, and do not invent new methodology, extra dimensions, or custom scoring rules.

## How to use this guide
- Start with the smallest truthful signal.
- Prefer the user's own words when they fit a dimension.
- Do not force every note into every dimension.
- If evidence is thin, say "no clear signal."
- If two dimensions fit, name both and explain why in one short line.
- Keep the interpretation narrow, conservative, and useful.

## The 8D abbreviations
- **PSY** = Psychological / emotional state
- **PHY** = Physical state and body recovery
- **ENV** = Environment and surrounding friction
- **SOC** = Social connection and relationship climate
- **SPI** = Spiritual meaning, purpose, values, alignment
- **INT** = Intellectual load, thinking, learning, focus
- **VOC** = Vocational output, execution, follow-through, momentum
- **FIN** = Financial pressure, resource stress, sustainability

## Human keyword cues
Use these as human phrases people actually say, not technical or AI-sounding labels.

### PSY
Positive cues: calm, good mood, steady, clear-headed, resilient, emotionally okay, at peace, less reactive, feeling lighter, in a good headspace, more grounded, doing better emotionally, feeling settled, handling things well, back to myself, more even-keeled
Negative cues: stressed, overwhelmed, anxious, on edge, burnt out, irritable, snappy, emotionally drained, fed up, sad, down, restless, discouraged, anxious about everything, can't shut my brain off, holding it together

### PHY
Positive cues: rested, slept well, woke up refreshed, good energy, feeling strong, recovered, nourished, hydrated, body feels good, moving better, less sore, got enough rest, feel human again, not dragging, bounced back, feeling physically okay
Negative cues: tired, wiped out, didn't sleep well, slept like crap, groggy, sore, achy, hungry, skipped meals, dehydrated, dragging, low energy, body feels heavy, feel run down, need rest, I'm not recovering

Positive cues: organized, calm space, everything in place, good setup, quiet, fewer distractions, my desk is clear, routine is working, my place feels good, easy to find things, smooth flow, less friction, workspace is helping, good rhythm, the environment is set up well

### SOC

Negative cues: lonely, left out, disconnected, arguing, tension, not feeling supported, feeling isolated, nobody gets it, relationship stress, family drama, friend stuff, not heard, feeling alone in it, distant, awkward, unsupported, not checked on

### SPI

### INT

### VOC

Negative cues: money stress, broke, bills piling up, paycheck to paycheck, can't afford it, spending too much, tight budget, worried about rent, financial pressure, cost of living, stretched thin, money feels tight, resources are low, trying to make it work, financial strain, I need to cut back

## Mapping notes
- A note about poor sleep and low energy usually maps first to **PHY**.
- A note about overwhelm, dread, or emotional friction usually maps first to **PSY**.
- A note about clutter, interruptions, or a bad setup usually maps first to **ENV**.
- A note about loneliness, conflict, or lack of support usually maps first to **SOC**.
- A note about meaning, purpose, or loss of direction usually maps first to **SPI**.
- A note about mental overload, reading trouble, or cognitive fatigue usually maps first to **INT**.
- A note about execution, procrastination, or follow-through usually maps first to **VOC**.
- A note about budget strain or resource pressure usually maps first to **FIN**.

## Guardrails
- Do not create a new taxonomy.
- Do not rename the dimensions.
- Do not add hidden scores or private calibration logic.
- Do not convert a keyword match into a diagnosis.
- Do not over-assign dimensions when one is enough.
- Do not claim certainty when the input is sparse.
